**Source:** [Birding via Facebook—Methodological Considerations When Crowdsourcing Observations of Bird Behavior via Social Media](https://www.mdpi.com/2673-6004/6/3/39)
- MDPI Journal Article

- Establishes Facebook as primary channel for Australian birding data sharing

## Research Methodology

### Search Strategy

This research employed a multi-phase search approach:

1. **Phase 1: Platform Communities** - Identified Australian birding Facebook groups, organizations, and communities
2. **Phase 2: Engagement Data** - Researched optimal posting times, engagement metrics, algorithm factors
3. **Phase 3: Operator Landscape** - Analyzed competitor social presence, messaging, content strategies
4. **Phase 4: Content Performance** - Gathered data on Instagram Reels, carousels, hashtag performance
5. **Phase 5: B2B Opportunities** - Researched LinkedIn tour operator landscape
6. **Phase 6: Resources & Legal** - Compiled stock image resources and licensing requirements

### Source Quality Assessment

**Sources Used (Confidence Levels):**
- **High Confidence:** Academic research (MDPI, Oxford Academic, Taylor & Francis), platform-official documentation (eBird, Unsplash, iStock)
- **Medium-High Confidence:** Industry-leading research platforms (Sprout Social, Hootsuite, Buffer, CreatorsJet, EpicOwl), Audubon Society
- **Medium Confidence:** Regional marketing agencies (Australian-based), influencer databases
- **Reference Quality:** Competitor websites, direct platform data
