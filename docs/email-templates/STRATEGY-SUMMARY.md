# Re-Engagement Email Series: Strategy Summary

## Campaign Objectives

**Primary Goal:** Reactivate users who signed up but never logged in

**Target Audience:** Birders aged 45-70
- High affinity for authentic communication
- Respond to real FOMO, not manipulative urgency
- Values community and quality experiences
- Willing to travel for rare species

**Success Metric:** 8%+ of re-engaged users log back in within 30 days of receiving first email

---

## Core Strategy: Authentic FOMO

### The Problem with Traditional Re-engagement

Most re-engagement campaigns use:
- Fake scarcity ("Only 3 spots left!")
- Manipulative urgency ("Offer ends tonight!")
- Generic messaging ("We miss you!")
- Discounts (destroying brand value)

**Why this fails with birders aged 45-70:**
- They've seen every marketing trick
- They value authenticity above all
- They're skeptical of hype
- They make deliberate, informed decisions

### Our Approach: Document Real Facts

**Instead of fake urgency, we provide:**
1. Real tours that actually ran
2. Real birds that were actually spotted
3. Real participant counts and sightings
4. Real photos and documentation
5. Specific regional data relevant to each user

**The FOMO is legitimate because:**
- It's rooted in factual tour data
- We're not inventing scarcity
- We're not using dark patterns
- We're honestly saying "these experiences happened without you"

## Email Series Arc

### Email #1 (30 days): "Welcome Back"
**Tone:** Helpful, gentle, low-pressure
**Hook Level:** Soft
**Message:** "Here's what's new, no pressure to use it"
**Psychology:** Reintroduce value proposition, remove friction

### Email #2 (90 days): "Regional Proof"
**Tone:** Direct, curious, data-driven
**Hook Level:** Medium
**Message:** "Look at what real birders found in your region"
**Psychology:** Build credibility with specific sightings, explain quorum system

### Email #3 (180 days): "Stark Reality"
**Tone:** Factual, honest FOMO, slightly confrontational
**Hook Level:** Strong
**Message:** "You missed these. Here's proof. Here's what could have been."
**Psychology:** Make opportunity cost real without being manipulative

### Email #4 (365 days): "Final Honesty"
**Tone:** Candid, frank, sympathetic but clear
**Hook Level:** Maximum (but factual)
**Message:** "A year is a long time. Here's everything you didn't do."
**Psychology:** Last chance to reactivate; honest about what they've missed

## Dynamic Personalization

Each email is personalized by:

1. **User Data**
   - First name
   - Region (Gulf Coast, Rocky Mountains, Northeast, etc.)
   - Signup date
   - Any chase list data if available

2. **Tour Data Specific to Their Region**
   - Count of tours added since signup
   - Count of tours in their specific region
   - Total tours user could have joined

3. **Sighting Data Specific to Their Region**
   - Top 2-3 birds spotted on tours in their region
   - Tour titles, dates, participant counts
   - Lifer count (potential new lifers in their region)
   - Any birds from their chase list that were spotted

4. **Year-in-Review Stats** (Email #4 only)
   - Total tours run (all regions)
   - Total sightings documented
   - Total lifers spotted
   - Their region's performance
   - Rarest sighting of the year

## Why This Campaign Works

### 1. Respects the Audience

**Principle:** No fake urgency, no dark patterns, no manipulation

The 45-70 demographic has built-in BS detectors. They:
- Ignore fake scarcity
- Resent dark patterns
- Appreciate honesty
- Value clear value propositions

**Our approach:** Give them real facts and let them decide

### 2. Provides Legitimate Proof

**Principle:** Every claim is backed by actual tour data

Instead of:
- "Great tours waiting for you!" (vague)
- "Birders love our platform!" (unsubstantiated)

We say:
- "8 people saw a Painted Bunting on May 15th on the Gulf Coast tour" (specific)
- "You could have been on that tour" (honest)
- "Here's the sighting report" (link to proof)

### 3. Escalates Gradually

**Principle:** Give multiple low-pressure opportunities before applying final pressure

- Email #1: Just FYI, here's what's new
- Email #2: Hey, your region had interesting tours
- Email #3: You actually missed these specific sightings
- Email #4: A whole year went by, and you missed 12 lifers

By Email #3, the recipient has had 6 months to act. The message is justified.

### 4. Acknowledges the Quorum System

**Principle:** Proactively explain why this platform is different

Birders might worry:
- "What if I commit and it cancels?"
- "Will I be forced to pay?"
- "Is this a sketchy platform?"

Email #2 and #3 explain:
- Tours only charge when they have confirmed groups
- You're never paying for a "might happen" trip
- This is actually better for birding than traditional platforms

### 5. Builds Narrative Arc

**Principle:** Tell a story across the series

- Email #1: "You've been away, but the platform evolved"
- Email #2: "Real tours ran in your region with real sightings"
- Email #3: "You literally could have seen these birds"
- Email #4: "A year passed and you missed everything"

Each email builds on the previous one. Recipient understands:
- What they missed (Email #1, #2)
- That it was real (Email #3)
- That it's now substantial (Email #4)

## Subject Line Strategy

### Email #1: Soft Hook
- A: Helpful tone ("{{user.first_name}}, {{tours.new_count}} new birding tours just arrived")
- B: Curious tone ("What's happening on Quorum Tours (you haven't seen yet)")
- C: Direct tone ("Your birding app is waiting")

**Why these work:** No urgency, no scarcity, pure information

### Email #2: Medium Hook
- A: Specific bird ("8 birders spotted {{sighting}} in {{region}}. You could have been there.")
- B: Data-driven ("{{tours.region_count}} tours ran in {{region}} while you were gone")
- C: Mild FOMO ("{{tours.region_count}} {{region}} tours. {{tours.region_count}} sightings you missed.")

**Why these work:** Introduce specific proof and mild regret language

### Email #3: Strong Hook
- A: Stark ("You missed {{lifers_count}} potential lifers in {{region}}. Here's proof.")
- B: Specific sighting ("A {{bird}} was spotted on {{date}}. {{participants}} people saw it. You didn't.")
- C: Numeric ("{{missed_tours}} tours. 0 with you on them.")

**Why these work:** Honest FOMO language but backed by real data

### Email #4: Maximum Hook
- A: Stark math ("12 lifers. 47 tours. 0 with you.")
- B: Year review ("Your year in review: 0 birds seen on Quorum Tours")
- C: Direct question ("One year later. Still haven't logged in?")

**Why these work:** This is the last email; honesty justified

## What NOT to Do

### Anti-Patterns This Campaign Avoids

❌ **No fake scarcity**
- "Only 2 spots left!" (unless literally true)
- "This offer expires tonight!" (unless it does)
- "These tours fill fast!" (unless actual queue exists)

❌ **No manufactured urgency**
- "Don't miss out!" (empty rhetoric)
- "Act now before it's too late!" (manipulative)
- "Limited time offer!" (dishonest if always available)

❌ **No dark patterns**
- Misleading unsubscribe location
- Auto-play videos with sound
- Trick CTAs ("Confirm Email" when clicking = sign-up)

❌ **No irrelevant content**
- Generic "we miss you" messaging
- Discounts (dilutes brand value for birders)
- Multiple CTAs (confuses intent)
- Overly designed (distracts from message)

## Compliance & Tone Guardrails

### Hard Rules (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Factual accuracy only**
   - Every statistic must be verifiable
   - No "might have been spotted" (only confirmed sightings)
   - No invented tour data

2. **Transparent intent**
   - Recipient knows this is a re-engagement campaign
   - We're clear we want them back
   - No deception about purpose

3. **Easy unsubscribe**
   - One-click unsubscribe in every email
   - Honor immediately (within 24 hours)
   - No re-enrollment tricks

4. **Respect boundaries**
   - Stop after Email #4 if user unsubscribes
   - No exceptions for "just one more"
   - Quarterly digest option (not automatic)

### Tone Guardrails

| Email | Min. Honesty | Max. FOMO | Override Point |
|-------|--------------|-----------|-----------------|
| #1 | Pure info | None | N/A |
| #2 | Factual | Mild regret | If complaint rate > 0.5% |
| #3 | Documented sightings | Stark ("You weren't there") | If complaint rate > 0.5% |
| #4 | Year-long facts | Maximum ("0 with you") | Campaign ends here |

## Questions & Support

### Common Questions

**Q: What if someone logs in between emails?**
A: Email #2-4 triggers will check `last_login_date` to prevent sending. If they logged in after Email #1, they won't get #2.

**Q: Can we personalize with chase list data?**
A: Yes, if chase list exists in `profiles` table. Falls back to region-based sightings if not.

**Q: How do we handle unsubscribes?**
A: Set `unsubscribe_reengagement = true` in profiles. All future emails skip this user.

**Q: Can we resend to someone who opened but didn't click?**
A: No. Once any email in the series is sent, we assume recipient received the message.

**Q: What about bounce handling?**
A: Resend handles bounces automatically. Monitor dashboard for hard bounces (>5% = investigate).
